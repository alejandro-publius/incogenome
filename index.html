DoseDNA

See how your DNA affects your medications.

Load a raw 23andMe or AncestryDNA file. DoseDNA reads it entirely in your browser and shows which common drugs your body processes differently — without your genome ever leaving your laptop.

On-device DNA parsing Only {gene, phenotype, drug} — plus the medications you type in — leaves your device
Informational only — not medical advice DoseDNA is a research-grade tool for understanding how your DNA may affect common medications. Always confirm any decision with a clinician or pharmacist before changing a prescription. Some genes (notably CYP2D6) can't be reliably called from consumer DNA arrays — those are flagged as coverage limited rather than guessed.

Load your DNA file

Select your raw 23andMe or AncestryDNA file. It reads in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

Supported formats: .txt .tsv .csv — the raw download from 23andMe or AncestryDNA. The bundled "Load sample file" is a small synthetic fixture for the demo.

Step 2

Your results

Per-gene phenotypes and the drugs they affect. Tap any drug for a plain-language explanation.

Load a DNA file above to see per-gene results.
Step 3

Medication check

Tell us what you take. We'll flag drug–gene conflicts, drug–drug interactions, and phenoconversion (when one drug changes how your body handles another).

Load your DNA above, then enter your medications to check for interactions.
Step 4

Prepare for your appointment

Generate specific questions to bring to your doctor or pharmacist, based on your results and meds.
