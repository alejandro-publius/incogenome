Plain language on the surface. Hard clinical data beneath.

Behind the conversation sits a deterministic engine: your file → diplotype → phenotype → a CPIC gene-drug row. Each answer is the output of that pipeline, phrased for you — not a model’s opinion about your health.

get_phenotype

Reads your status

Finds the variants in your file, resolves the diplotype, and returns your metabolizer type — or “not determined.”

check_drug_gene

Maps drug to risk

Pairs a medication with the CYP gene that handles it and the direction of effect — too little, or too much.

check_phenoconversion

Catches interactions

Flags when another drug you take could blunt an enzyme and shift your effective phenotype.

add_med

Builds your list

Keeps the medications you mention this session so it can reason across them — locally, never stored remotely.

One status. Two drugs. Opposite advice.

Your CYP450 enzymes process nearly every prescription drug. The same slow enzyme helps one medication and harms another — so a single “normal/abnormal” label is dangerously incomplete.

Your DNA is read here, and stays here.

Selecting a file is not uploading it. DoseDNA parses your data in a Web Worker on your device. The clinical engine runs offline; the only thing that can ever leave is a de-identified, three-field question — and only if you opt in to nicer wording.
